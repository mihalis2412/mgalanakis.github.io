Master Thesis

Abstract: Survival extrapolation can be potentially dangerous since one may not make such predictions with pre-specified statistical accuracy. However, such extrapolation necessarily takes place implicitly or explicitly in a number of scientific fields, including in health economic evaluations. This thesis will investigate techniques for survival extrapolation using suitable richly parametric, semi-parametric and machine learning methods for time to event data. Particular attention will be placed in the statistical and computational efficiency of the distinct methods involved and how synthesizing different sources of evidence may improve upon the predictive ability.
